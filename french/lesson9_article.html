Lesson 9: 🧭 Directions & Time in French

Learn how to say directions and parts of the day in simple French. Each word is explained in English!

🗺️ Directions in French

These words help you ask or give directions when traveling:

  • le nord – north
  • le sud – south
  • l’est – east
  • l’ouest – west
  • la direction – the direction

🕰️ Times of Day in French

Learn how to say different parts of the day:

  • le matin – morning
  • le midi – noon / midday
  • l’après-midi – afternoon
  • le soir – evening
  • la nuit – night

💡 Grammar & How to Use

  • le / la / l’ = "the" in French. Use le, la, and l’ before vowels.
  • à = to / at → used for directions and time.
  • en = in → often used for places or seasons.

📘 Easy Example Sentences

Je vais au nord .
I’m going to the north.
👉 Use à + le = au when saying “to the” for masculine nouns like le nord.

Le soir, je regarde la télé.
In the evening, I watch TV.
👉 Just say the part of the day directly: le matin, le soir, etc.

La boulangerie est à l’ouest .
The bakery is to the west.
👉 Use à l’ when the word starts with a vowel like ouest.

💭 Did You Know?

  • 🌅 le matin , le soir and le midi are all masculine.
  • 🌙 la nuit is the only one that is feminine (so it uses la).
  • 📍 direction = “direction” and is feminine → la direction
  • 🗺️ To say “towards Paris” → en direction de Paris
  • 🕒 You can greet people based on time:
    – Bonjour = Good morning / Hello
    – Bonsoir = Good evening
    – Bonne nuit = Good night
