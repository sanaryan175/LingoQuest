Lesson 3: Family in Spanish

Learn how to talk about your family and loved ones in Spanish.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Key Family Vocabulary

  • La familia – Family
  • El padre – Father
  • La madre – Mother
  • El hermano – Brother
  • La hermana – Sister
  • Los padres – Parents
  • El hijo – Son
  • La hija – Daughter
  • Los abuelos – Grandparents
  • El abuelo – Grandfather
  • La abuela – Grandmother
  • El tío – Uncle
  • La tía – Aunt
  • El primo – Male cousin
  • La prima – Female cousin
  • El niño – Boy / child
  • La niña – Girl / child
  • Los niños – Children

📚 Grammar Tips

  • Gender & Articles: Masculine → el , Feminine → la . Plural masculine or mixed → los , plural feminine → las .
  • Plural Form: Usually add -s if the word ends in a vowel, and -es if it ends in a consonant.
    Examples:
    La madre → Las madres
    El padre → Los padres
  • Possessive Pronouns: "Mi padre" (my father), "Mi madre" (my mother). Plural: "Mis padres" (my parents).
  • Verb Agreement: “Mis padres son amables” (My parents are kind).

💬 Usage in Context – Talking About Family

Tengo un hermano y una hermana.
I have a brother and a sister.

Mi padre es médico.
My father is a doctor.

Mis abuelos viven en Madrid.
My grandparents live in Madrid.

¿Cómo se llama tu madre?
What is your mother’s name?

Mi familia es muy grande.
My family is very big.

🧠 Fun Facts

  • In Spanish, it’s common to use affectionate terms like mamá (mom) and papá (dad).
  • Spanish family words can vary between countries — for example, “padre” in Spain is sometimes replaced with “papá,” and “hermano” may be “bro” in informal speech.
  • The word familia is feminine, so we say la familia.
