Lesson 1: Greetings in Spanish

Begin your Spanish journey with the most common ways to greet and say goodbye.

👋 Common Spanish Greetings

Here are the most common greetings you'll hear in Spanish-speaking countries:

  • Hola – Hello
  • Buenos días – Good morning
  • Buenas tardes – Good afternoon
  • Buenas noches – Good evening / Good night
  • ¿Cómo estás? – How are you? (informal)
  • ¿Cómo está usted? – How are you? (formal)
  • ¿Qué tal? – How’s it going?

🙋 Saying Goodbye

  • Adiós – Goodbye
  • Chao – Bye (casual)
  • Hasta luego – See you later
  • Hasta pronto – See you soon
  • Nos vemos – See you

📘 Example Conversations

Person A: Good morning! How are you?
¡Buenos días! ¿Cómo estás?

Person B: I’m good, thank you. And you?
Estoy bien, gracias. ¿Y tú?

Person A: Bye!
¡Adiós!

Person B: See you soon!
¡Hasta pronto!

💭 Did You Know?

  • 👋 In Spain, friends often greet with a kiss on both cheeks — even between men and women.
  • 🌎 In Latin America, handshakes are more common for first meetings.
  • ⏰ "Buenos días" is used until lunchtime, then switch to "Buenas tardes".
  • 🌙 Use "Buenas noches" both for greeting at night and for saying goodnight.
