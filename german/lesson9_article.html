Lesson 9: 🧭 Directions & Time in German

Learn how to talk about directions and different times of the day in German.

🗺️ Directions in German

Knowing how to give or understand directions is useful when traveling or navigating a city:

  • Norden – North
  • Süden – South
  • Osten – East
  • Westen – West
  • Richtung – Direction

🕰️ Times of Day

Let’s go over how to say different times of the day in German:

  • Morgen – Morning
  • Mittag – Midday / Noon
  • Nachmittag – Afternoon
  • Abend – Evening
  • Nacht – Night

💡 Grammar & Sentence Usage

  • “im” is commonly used before time periods (e.g., im Morgen, im Abend).
  • Prepositions like nach (to), von (from), and in (in) are often used when giving directions.
  • Die Richtung is feminine — always use die.

📘 Example Sentences

Ich gehe nach Norden. – I’m going north.

Am Abend esse ich mit meiner Familie. – In the evening, I eat with my family.

Der Supermarkt ist im Westen. – The supermarket is in the west.

💭 Did You Know?

  • 🕓 In German, time expressions are often paired with the article “der” (e.g., der Morgen, der Abend), except die Nacht, which is feminine.
  • 🧭 The word “Richtung” (direction) is commonly used in everyday speech and also in signs, like Richtung Berlin = “towards Berlin”.
  • 🗺️ Compass directions (Norden, Süden, etc.) are all **masculine nouns** and often used with prepositions like im (in the), nach (to), or aus (from).
  • 🌄 “Morgen” means both “morning” and “tomorrow”. You can tell the difference by the context: am Morgen = in the morning, bis morgen = see you tomorrow.
  • 🎯 Germans often use time words in greetings too: Guten Morgen (Good morning), Guten Abend (Good evening), Gute Nacht (Good night).
