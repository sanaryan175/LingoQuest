Lesson 11: 📚 Grammar Basics – 2

Learn about articles, plurals, negation, and imperative forms to strengthen your German sentence skills.

📖 Articles and Plural Forms

German has definite (the) and indefinite (a/an) articles. They change depending on the gender (masculine, feminine, neuter) and case.

  • Masculine: der (the), ein (a)
    Example: der Mann (the man)
  • Feminine: die (the), eine (a)
    Example: die Frau (the woman)
  • Neuter: das (the), ein (a)
    Example: das Kind (the child)

Plural: All plural nouns take die as the definite article.
Example: die Kinder (the children)

🧩 Plural formation varies:
- Add -e: der Tisch → die Tische
- Add -er: das Kind → die Kinder
- Add -n/-en: die Frau → die Frauen

🚫 Negation (kein, nicht)

German uses kein and nicht to say “no” or “not”.

  • kein negates nouns without an article or with an indefinite article:
    Example: Ich habe kein Auto. (I don’t have a car)
  • nicht negates verbs, adjectives, or specific parts of a sentence:
    Example: Ich spiele nicht. (I’m not playing)

🧩 Position rule:
- nicht usually comes at the end of a clause unless it modifies a specific word.

📢 Imperative Forms

The imperative is used for giving commands, instructions, or suggestions.

  • du-form (informal, singular): Remove the -st ending from the verb.
    Example: Geh! (Go!)
  • ihr-form (informal, plural): Use the regular ihr form.
    Example: Geht! (Go! – to several people)
  • Sie-form (formal): Invert the verb and pronoun.
    Example: Gehen Sie! (Go! – polite)

💡 To soften a command, add bitte (please) at the start or end:
Bitte kommen Sie. (Please come.)
