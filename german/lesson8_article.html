Lesson 8: Food and Drinks in German

Let’s explore how to talk about meals, snacks, and beverages in German!

🍽️ Basic Food Vocabulary

  • das Brot – bread
  • der Käse – cheese
  • die Suppe – soup
  • das Fleisch – meat
  • das Gemüse – vegetables
  • das Obst – fruits
  • die Kartoffel – potato
  • der Reis – rice

🥤 Common Drinks

  • das Wasser – water
  • der Saft – juice
  • die Milch – milk
  • der Kaffee – coffee
  • der Tee – tea
  • die Limonade – lemonade

💡 Grammar & Sentence Structure

  • Ich esse... – I eat...
  • Ich trinke... – I drink...
  • Ich habe Hunger – I am hungry
  • Ich habe Durst – I am thirsty
  • Der/Die/Das are articles based on gender of the noun (der Saft, die Suppe, das Wasser).

📘 Example Sentences

Ich esse gerne Brot und Käse. – I like eating bread and cheese.

Ich trinke jeden Morgen Kaffee. – I drink coffee every morning.

Wir haben Suppe zum Mittagessen. – We have soup for lunch.

Magst du Obst und Gemüse? – Do you like fruits and vegetables?

💭 Did You Know?

  • 📚 In German, all nouns are capitalized! For example, it's das Brot (the bread), not das brot. This rule applies to every noun, always.
  • 🧠 German articles depend on the gender of the noun:
    • der – masculine (e.g., der Käse 🧀 – the cheese)
    • die – feminine (e.g., die Suppe 🍲 – the soup)
    • das – neuter (e.g., das Wasser 💧 – the water)
    Learning the article with the noun helps with grammar later!
  • 🍽️ A polite way to ask for food or drink is: Ich hätte gern... which means “I would like...” – it’s commonly used in restaurants and cafes.
  • 🥦 Want to say you're vegetarian in German? Say: Ich bin Vegetarier (if you're male) or Ich bin Vegetarierin (if you're female).
  • 🗣️ If you're offering something, use Möchtest du...? which means “Would you like...?” – perfect for friendly conversations involving snacks or drinks!
  • 🧊 Germans often drink **sparkling water** (called Sprudelwasser) more than still water. So if you want plain water, ask for stilles Wasser!
