Lesson 5: Days, Months & Seasons in German

Master how to talk about days, months, and seasons in German — essential for real-life conversations!

📅 Days of the Week (Wochentage)

  • Montag – Monday
  • Dienstag – Tuesday
  • Mittwoch – Wednesday
  • Donnerstag – Thursday
  • Freitag – Friday
  • Samstag – Saturday
  • Sonntag – Sunday

Note: Days are **not capitalized** in English, but in German, **all nouns are capitalized**.

🗓️ Months of the Year (Monate)

  • Januar – January
  • Februar – February
  • März – March
  • April – April
  • Mai – May
  • Juni – June
  • Juli – July
  • August – August
  • September – September
  • Oktober – October
  • November – November
  • Dezember – December

🌤️ Seasons (Jahreszeiten)

  • Der Frühling – Spring
  • Der Sommer – Summer
  • Der Herbst – Autumn/Fall
  • Der Winter – Winter

🧠 Grammar & Sentence Structure – Talking About Time in German

  • 📅 How to say "on Monday" or "on Friday"
    Use am before days of the week.
    ➤ Am Montag gehe ich zur Schule.
    (I go to school on Monday)
    ✅ “am” = “on” for days.
  • 📆 How to say "in December" or "in summer"
    Use im before months and seasons.
    ➤ Im Dezember schneit es oft.
    (It often snows in December)
    ➤ Im Sommer fahren wir ans Meer.
    (In summer, we go to the sea)
    ✅ “im” = “in the” for months/seasons.
  • 👨‍🏫 What gender are days, months, and seasons?
    In German, they are all **masculine**, so they use der:
    ➤ der Montag (Monday), der Juli (July), der Winter (winter)
    ✅ Just remember: "der" for days, months & seasons!
  • 🔁 How to say "every Friday" or "every Monday"
    Use jeden before the day:
    ➤ Jeden Freitag spiele ich Fußball.
    (I play football every Friday)
    ✅ “jeden” = “every” (for masculine nouns like weekdays).
  • 📅 How to say a date like "October 3rd"
    Use am + ordinal number + month:
    ➤ Am dritten Oktober ist ein Feiertag.
    (On October 3rd, there's a holiday)
    ✅ Example format: “am ersten Januar”, “am fünften Mai”
  • 🍂 More examples with seasons:
    ➤ Der Herbst ist kühl und windig.
    (Autumn is cool and windy)
    ➤ Im Frühling blühen die Blumen.
    (In spring, the flowers bloom)
  • 🌞 How to say times of day like "in the morning"
    Again, use am:
    ➤ am Morgen – in the morning
    ➤ am Abend – in the evening
    ✅ Use “am” for parts of the day too!

💬 Usage in Context – Talking About Days, Months & Seasons

Heute ist Dienstag.
Today is Tuesday.
📅 Heute = Today, ist = is, Dienstag = Tuesday

Mein Geburtstag ist im April.
My birthday is in April.
🎂 Use im for months → im + [month]

Wir reisen gern im Sommer.
We love traveling in summer.
✈️ Wir = we, reisen = travel, gern = like to, im Sommer = in summer

Am Sonntag bleibe ich zu Hause.
I stay home on Sunday.
🏡 Use am for days → am + [day]

Der Herbst ist meine Lieblingsjahreszeit.
Autumn is my favorite season.
🍁 Herbst = Autumn, Lieblingsjahreszeit = favorite season (long word, but fun!)

🌍 Did You Know? – Fun Facts About Days & Seasons in Germany

  • 📅 The German week starts on Monday, not Sunday!
    So if you're planning your week in Germany, remember: Montag (Monday) is day 1!
  • 🎉 Seasons are important for German culture!
    Many festivals happen in specific seasons, like Oktoberfest in Herbst (Autumn), and Weihnachtsmärkte (Christmas markets) in Winter.
  • 🗓️ German calendars use short forms for weekdays:
    Mo (Montag), Di (Dienstag), Mi (Mittwoch), Do (Donnerstag), Fr (Freitag), Sa (Samstag), So (Sonntag)
    ✅ You’ll see these everywhere—from school schedules to train timetables!
