Lesson 5: Days, Months & Seasons in German

Master how to talk about days, months, and seasons in German — essential for real-life conversations!

📅 Days of the Week (Wochentage)

  • Montag – Monday
  • Dienstag – Tuesday
  • Mittwoch – Wednesday
  • Donnerstag – Thursday
  • Freitag – Friday
  • Samstag – Saturday
  • Sonntag – Sunday

Note: Days are **not capitalized** in English, but in German, **all nouns are capitalized**.

🗓️ Months of the Year (Monate)

  • Januar – January
  • Februar – February
  • März – March
  • April – April
  • Mai – May
  • Juni – June
  • Juli – July
  • August – August
  • September – September
  • Oktober – October
  • November – November
  • Dezember – December

🌤️ Seasons (Jahreszeiten)

  • Der Frühling – Spring
  • Der Sommer – Summer
  • Der Herbst – Autumn/Fall
  • Der Winter – Winter

🧠 Grammar & Sentence Structure

  • Days of the week use the preposition am (on):
    Am Montag gehe ich zur Schule. – I go to school on Monday.
  • Months and seasons use im (in the):
    Im Dezember schneit es oft. – It often snows in December.
  • All days, months, and seasons are masculine nouns, so we use der:
    der Montag, der Juli, der Winter
  • To express something happens regularly, use "jeden" (every):
    Jeden Freitag spiele ich Fußball. – I play football every Friday.
  • To talk about dates, use:
    am + [ordinal number]. [month]
    Am dritten Oktober ist ein Feiertag. – October 3rd is a holiday.
  • Seasonal phrases:
    • Im Sommer fahren wir ans Meer. – In summer we go to the sea.
    • Der Herbst ist kühl und windig. – Autumn is cool and windy.
  • To say “in the morning / evening” etc., use:
    • am Morgen – in the morning
    • am Abend – in the evening

💬 Usage in Context

Heute ist Dienstag. – Today is Tuesday.

Mein Geburtstag ist im April. – My birthday is in April.

Wir reisen gern im Sommer. – We love traveling in summer.

Am Sonntag bleibe ich zu Hause. – I stay home on Sunday.

Der Herbst ist meine Lieblingsjahreszeit. – Autumn is my favorite season.

🌍 Fun Facts

  • In Germany, the week starts on **Monday**, not Sunday!
  • Seasons play a big cultural role: many festivals like **Oktoberfest** happen in **Herbst** (Autumn).
  • In German calendars, days are often abbreviated as Mo, Di, Mi, Do, Fr, Sa, So.
