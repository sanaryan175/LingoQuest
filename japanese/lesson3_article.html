Lesson 3: Family in Japanese

Learn how to talk about your family and loved ones in Japanese.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Key Family Vocabulary

  • 家族 (かぞく, kazoku) – Family
  • 父 (ちち, chichi) – My father
  • 母 (はは, haha) – My mother
  • お父さん (おとうさん, otōsan) – Father (respectful)
  • お母さん (おかあさん, okāsan) – Mother (respectful)
  • 兄 (あに, ani) – My older brother
  • お兄さん (おにいさん, onīsan) – Older brother (respectful)
  • 姉 (あね, ane) – My older sister
  • お姉さん (おねえさん, onēsan) – Older sister (respectful)
  • 弟 (おとうと, otōto) – Younger brother
  • 妹 (いもうと, imōto) – Younger sister
  • 祖父 (そふ, sofu) – My grandfather
  • お祖父さん (おじいさん, ojīsan) – Grandfather (respectful)
  • 祖母 (そぼ, sobo) – My grandmother
  • お祖母さん (おばあさん, obāsan) – Grandmother (respectful)
  • 叔父/伯父 (おじ, oji) – Uncle
  • 叔母/伯母 (おば, oba) – Aunt
  • 子供 (こども, kodomo) – Child
  • 兄弟 (きょうだい, kyōdai) – Siblings

📚 Grammar Tips

  • Respect Levels: Use respectful terms (with お/ご prefixes) when speaking about someone else’s family, but humble forms for your own.
  • Plural: Japanese usually doesn’t have separate plural forms; context tells if it’s plural. Sometimes ～たち (tachi) is added (e.g., 子供たち – children).
  • Particles: "の" indicates possession – e.g., 私の母 (my mother).
  • Topic Markers: Use は after the topic – e.g., 母は先生です (My mother is a teacher).

💬 Usage in Context

私の家族は5人です。 – My family has five members.

兄は東京に住んでいます。 – My older brother lives in Tokyo.

お母さんは料理が上手です。 – My mother is good at cooking.

祖父と祖母は元気です。 – My grandfather and grandmother are healthy.

弟の名前はケンです。 – My younger brother’s name is Ken.

🧠 Fun Facts

  • Japanese has different words for talking about your own family vs. someone else’s, to show respect.
  • Politeness levels are very important; even family terms can change depending on formality.
  • Many families use casual nicknames like お父ちゃん or お母ちゃん in informal settings.
