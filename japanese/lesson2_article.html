Lesson 2: Numbers in Japanese

Learn how to count and use numbers in daily Japanese situations.

🔢 Numbers from 0 to 25

  • 0 – ゼロ (Zero)
  • 1 – 一 (いち / Ichi)
  • 2 – 二 (に / Ni)
  • 3 – 三 (さん / San)
  • 4 – 四 (よん / Yon)

🎯 Milestone Numbers

  • 30 – 三十 (さんじゅう / Sanjuu)
  • 40 – 四十 (よんじゅう / Yonjuu)
  • 50 – 五十 (ごじゅう / Gojuu)
  • 100 – 百 (ひゃく / Hyaku)
  • 1000 – 千 (せん / Sen)
  • 10,000 – 一万 (いちまん / Ichiman)

💡 Grammar & Number Rules

  • Japanese numbers stack simply using place values: e.g., 二十一 = 21 ("2 tens + 1").
  • Hyaku (100) and Sen (1000) are standalone but combine easily: e.g., 三百 (300).
  • The number 4 can also be read as "し (shi)", and 7 as "しち (shichi)", but “よん” and “なな” are preferred to avoid confusion.

📘 Usage in Context

私は二十歳です。 – Watashi wa hatachi desu. → I am 20 years old.

それは五百円です。 – Sore wa gohyaku en desu. → That is 500 yen.

三人が来ました。 – San-nin ga kimashita. → Three people came.

部屋は十番です。 – Heya wa juu-ban desu. → The room is number 10.

彼は一万人のフォロワーがいます。 – Kare wa ichiman nin no forowā ga imasu. → He has 10,000 followers.

🧠 Fun Facts

  • Japanese uses different counters for objects (e.g., "〜人" for people, "〜本" for long objects).
  • The number 4 (し / shi) sounds like "death", so it's often avoided in hospitals and hotels.
  • Japanese prices usually end in 〜円 (yen). Example: 850円 = Happyaku gojuu en.
