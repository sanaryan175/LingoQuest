Lesson 4: Hobbies in Japanese

Let's learn how to talk about our hobbies in Japanese — fun things like reading, sports, and music!

🎨 Hobbies Vocabulary (Words)

Here are common Japanese words for hobbies:

  • しゅみ (趣味) – Hobby
  • おんがくをきく (音楽を聴く) – Listen to music
  • サッカーをする – Play soccer
  • ほんをよむ (本を読む) – Read books
  • えをかく (絵を描く) – Draw pictures
  • おどる (踊る) – Dance
  • およぐ (泳ぐ) – Swim
  • しゃしんをとる (写真を撮る) – Take photos
  • りょこうする (旅行する) – Travel
  • りょうりする (料理する) – Cook
  • えんげい (園芸) – Gardening
  • ゲームをする – Play games
  • ジョギングする – Jogging

📚 How to Say “I Like...”

In Japanese, to say “I like something”, we use:

  • ～が好きです (ga suki desu) = “I like ~”

Example:

サッカーが好きです。
(Sakkā ga suki desu.) → I like soccer.

If you want to say “I like doing something”, use:

  • ～することが好きです = “I like to do ~”

Example:

りょうりすることが好きです。
(Ryōri suru koto ga suki desu.) → I like cooking.

🧩 Word Order & Particles

Japanese sentences usually follow this order:

Topic + Object + Verb

We use the particle を (wo) for objects of action:

  • おんがくをきく – Listen to music
  • ゲームをする – Play games

Examples:

私は音楽を聴くのが好きです。
(Watashi wa ongaku o kiku no ga suki desu.) → I like listening to music.

弟はゲームをするのが大好きです。
(Otōto wa gēmu o suru no ga daisuki desu.) → My younger brother loves playing games.

💬 How to Talk About Your Hobbies

When you want to ask someone about their hobbies, say:

しゅみはなんですか？
(Shumi wa nan desu ka?) – What is your hobby?

Example answers:

私はほんをよむのが好きです。
(Watashi wa hon o yomu no ga suki desu.) – I like reading books.

週末に旅行します。
(Shūmatsu ni ryokō shimasu.) – I travel on weekends.

母はえをかくのが好きです。
(Haha wa e o kaku no ga suki desu.) – My mother likes drawing.

🧠 Did You Know?

  • “しゅみ (shumi)” is used a lot in Japanese conversations. People love asking about hobbies!
  • Japanese schools have clubs for every hobby – music, anime, sports, gardening, and more!
  • To change an action (like reading) into a noun, you can add こと or の.
  • Example: よむ → よむこと (yomu koto) or よむの (yomu no) = “reading”
